Blueberry Pie Recipe

Description

This is a delicious blueberry pie recipe made Sally McKenney at Sally's Baking Addiction.

Ingredients

  • Homemade Pie Crust or All Butter Pie Crust
  • 6 cups fresh blueberries
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
  • egg wash: 1 large egg beaten with 1 tablespoon milk
  • optional: coarse sugar for sprinkling on crust

Steps

  1. Prepare either pie crust recipe through step 5.
  2. In a large bowl, stir together blueberries, sugar, flour, cornstarch, and cinnamon. Mix until no longer dry—burst a few blueberries if needed to moisten. Set aside while oven preheats. (Filling can be refrigerated for up to 24 hours.)
  3. Adjust rack to the lower third and preheat to 425°F (218°C). Place a baking sheet on the bottom rack to catch any drips.
  4. On a floured surface, roll out one disc of dough into a 12-inch circle. Place in a 9 x 1.5 or 2-inch pie dish and smooth it in. Add filling and dot with small pieces of butter. Set aside.
  5. Roll out second disc of dough into a 12-inch circle. Cut into strips and weave a lattice over the pie. Press edges to seal, trim excess dough, and crimp or flute the edges.
  6. Brush top and edges with egg wash. Sprinkle with coarse sugar, if using.
  7. Bake at 425°F for 25 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) and bake 40–50 minutes more, until the filling bubbles in the center and reaches ~200°F (93°C). Use a crust shield to prevent over-browning. Tent with foil if top browns too quickly.
  8. Cool on a rack for at least 4 hours before slicing. Store leftovers tightly covered in the fridge for up to 5 days.
